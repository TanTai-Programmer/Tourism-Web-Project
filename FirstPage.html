Ha Long Bay

Ha Long tourism is known as a beautiful ink painting in Quang Ninh. This place attracts millions of visitors every year, both domestic and foreign tourists. When it comes to attractive tourist destinations in Vietnam, Ha Long is definitely a place chosen by many tourists. Because of the beautiful, majestic and unique natural scenery with many impressive caves and historical monuments. Ha Long Bay is one of the top beautiful bays in the world with dreamy scenery, truly a masterpiece of nature. Ha Long Bay is a small bay on the west coast of the Gulf of Tonkin in Vietnam. Including the sea and islands of Ha Long city in Quang Ninh province. Ha Long Bay has an area of about 1,553 square kilometers, including 1,969 large and small islands. Most of them are limestone islands, in which the core area of the bay has an area of 335km2, with a dense concentration of 775 islands.

Son Dong Cave

Son Doong Cave has coordinates 17°55'N and 106°14'E, located in Son Trach commune, Bo Trach district, Quang Binh province. Son Doong Cave is located in the core zone of Phong Nha Ke Bang National Park. The cave is located close to the Vietnam-Laos border. Twice recognized by UNESCO as a World Natural Heritage Site. Son Doong Cave is located in Bo Trach district, Quang Binh province. Son Doong Cave was formed about 2-5 million years ago, when river water flowed through the limestone area that had been buried along a fault line. The water flow eroded and created a giant tunnel in the ground under the mountain range. In places where the rock is soft, the ceiling collapses to form sinkholes, which over time form giant cave arches. The cave has a width of 150 meters, a height of more than 200 meters, a length of up to nearly 9km. Estimated capacity of Son Doong Cave is 38.5 million cubic meters (equivalent to 15,000 Olympic swimming pools).
